What is Gross Profit ? Formula & Explanation with Examples

Gross Profit is the money you or your company has left after substracting cost of goods / investment from revenue (or sale amount).

Example 1 : You bought a company stock at the price of 100$, and buying a total of 100 shares.

So, the cost is = 100 x 100 = 10000$ (Cost).

And after 2 years say you decided to sell your position in the company and the current share price is 130$ per share.

So, the amount you will recieve from sale of 100 share is = 100 x 130 = 13000$ (Recievables from Sale)

Formula : Gross Profit = Total Recievables from Sale - Total Cost

So, Gross Profit = 13000 - 10000 = 3000$
